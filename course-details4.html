Bachelor of Electronic Engineering with Honours

Introduction

This course is designed for those who wish to broaden their knowledge in electronics engineering. Itâ€™s main objectives are to train students in the aspects of analysis and design, practical and project handling and problem solving skills.

In doing so, we hope our graduates will be equipped with a solid foundation upon joining employment and with the necessary skills to meet the challenges in the fields of engineering today.

Programme Objectives (POs)

The objectives of the programme are:

PEO 1: Graduates who are competent and productive in satisfying customer needs.

PEO 2: Graduates grow professionally and engage in lifelong learning.

PEO 3: Graduates demonstrate professionalism in engineering practice and ethical conduct, awareness in sustainability issues and contribute to multi-cultural society.

Program Learning Outcomes (PLOs)

Our programme learning outcomes are listed as follows:

PLO 1. Engineering Knowledge: Ability to apply acquired fundamental knowledge of mathematics, science and electronic engineering to the solution of complex engineering problems.

PLO 2. Problem Analysis: Able to identify, formulate, research, analysis and synthesis of conclusions for the complex engineering problems.

PLO 3. Design/Development of Solutions: Design solutions for complex engineering problems and systems, component or processes that meet specified needs with appropriate consideration for public health and safety, multi-cultural society and environment.

PLO 4. Investigation: Ability to conduct investigation into complex problems using research-based knowledge and research methods including data analysis and interpretation to obtain valid conclusions.

PLO 5. Modern Tool Usage: Ability to create, select and apply appropriate techniques, resources, modern engineering and IT tools to complex engineering practice , with an understanding of the limitations.

PLO 6. The Engineer and Society: Understand the role of engineers in multi-cultural society, especially the health, safety, legal and cultural issues and the consequent responsibilities relevant to professional engineering.

PLO 7. Ethics: Understand the role of engineers in multi-cultural society, especially the health, safety, legal and cultural issues and the consequent responsibilities relevant to professional engineering.

PLO 8. Environment and Sustainability: Understand the needs of sustainability development and the impact of professional engineering solutions to society and environment.

PLO 9. Individual and Team Work: Ability to function effectively as an individual, and as a member or leader in a team with multi-cultural background.

PLO 10. Communication: Ability to communicate effectively on complex engineering activities in both written and verbally with the engineering community and with multi-cultural society at large.

PLO 11. Life Long Learning: Recognize the need for, and have the preparation and ability to engage in independent and life-long learning in the fast electronic technological change.

PLO 12. Project Management and Finance: Demonstrate knowledge and understanding of engineering and management principles to manage projects as a member and a leader of a team, in multidisciplinary environments.

Entry Requirements

1. STPM/ A Level: full passes in 2 subjects include Mathematics and Physics / Chemistry
2. UEC: at least 5 credits include Mathematics and Physics / Chemistry

3. Matriculation/ Pre-University that recognized by the MOE: at least a CGPA of 2.0

4. Foundation programs in relevant field from any HEPs recognized by MOE: at least CGPAs of 2.0

5. Other recognized equivalent qualifications

SUC Bachelor of Electronic Engineering with Honours Accredited by EAC

Our Bachelor degree programme is accredited by the Engineering Accreditation Council (EAC) and recognized by the Malaysian Government. EAC is a delegated body by the Board of Engineers Malaysia (BEM) as the only recognized accrediting body for engineering degree programmes offered in Malaysia.

This degree is also internationally recognised under the Washington Accord, a multinational agreement which recognises the substantial equivalency of engineering degree programmes accredited by the responsible bodies in each of the current signatory countries.

List of Signatories Countries: Australia, Canada, Hong Kong China, India, Ireland, Japan, Korea, Malaysia, New Zealand, Russia, Singapore, South Africa, Sri Lanka, Turkey, United Kingdom, United States

Link of for list of university accredited by EAC: http://www.eac.org.my/web/list_accredited.htm
